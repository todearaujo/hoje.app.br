## NL;NL

- **A nova tendência**: O "analog bag" (bolsa analógica) está ganhando popularidade entre a Geração Z e millennials como uma ferramenta de bem-estar para combater o vício em telas e o "doomscrolling".
- **Como funciona**: Consiste em carregar uma bolsa com atividades práticas e táteis — como livros, cadernos, materiais de pintura, tricô ou câmeras instantâneas — para substituir o impulso de pegar o celular durante momentos de tédio.
- **A ciência do hábito**: Segundo especialistas, a estratégia funciona porque altera o ciclo do hábito: ao substituir o gatilho (tédio) e a recompensa (entretenimento) por uma nova atividade física e próxima, torna-se mais fácil romper o ciclo do uso constante de telas.
- **O contexto**: O movimento faz parte de uma busca mais ampla pelo "bem-estar analógico", valorizando experiências de criação e desconexão digital em um mundo cada vez mais tecnológico.

# Analog bags are in. Doomscrolling is out.

The latest must-have accessory is a " [stop-scrolling](https://www.axios.com/2025/09/26/phone-use-morning-finding-focus) bag" — a tote packed with analog activities like watercolors and crossword puzzles.

**Why it matters:** We spend hours glued to our [screens](https://www.npr.org/2025/02/24/nx-s1-5304417/smartphone-break-digital-detox-screen-addiction). "Analog bags," as they're also called, are one way millennials and Gen Zers are reclaiming that time.

**How it works:** "I basically just put everything I could grab for instead of my phone into a bag," including knitting, a scrapbook and a Polaroid camera, says [Sierra Campbell](https://www.tiktok.com/@siececampbell/video/7543686560848366879?lang=en), the content creator behind the trend.

- The 31-year-old keeps one bag at home in Northern California, carrying it from room to room, and another in her car.

**State of play:** The trend has quickly spread on social media, part of a bigger shift to unplug.

- Roughly 1,600 TikTok posts were tagged #AnalogLife during the first nine months of 2025 — up over 330% from the same period last year, according to TikTok data shared with Axios.

**The intrigue:** Science explains why we keep reaching for our phones — and why carrying something else, even a single book, can help us stop.

- Scrolling, like other habits, is driven by a cue (boredom) and a reward (entertainment), says Charles Duhigg, bestselling author of " [The Power of Habit](https://charlesduhigg.com/the-power-of-habit/)."
- "If we want to change that \[habit\], we have to … find a new behavior that corresponds to that old cue and delivers something similar to that old reward," he tells Axios.
- Keeping your new habit within arm's reach makes the swap easier.

**The big picture:** Stop-scrolling bags fit into a broader revival of analog hobbies, led by younger people, that researchers say is less about trendy [nostalgia](https://www.axios.com/2025/09/10/gen-z-fashion-y2k) than embracing a [pre-digital](https://www.fastcompany.com/90909279/gen-xers-and-older-millennials-really-just-want-to-go-back-in-time-to-before-the-internet-existed), pre-AI world.

**What we're hearing:** "It speaks to an incredible desperation and desire for experiences that return our attention to us, that fight [brain-rotting](https://www.axios.com/2024/12/02/brain-rot-meaning-word-of-the-year), that are [tactile](https://www.axios.com/2025/09/28/mahjong-teacher-lesson) … that involve [creating](https://www.axios.com/2024/12/07/needlepoint-grandma-hobbies-tiktok) over scrolling," Beth McGroarty, vice president of research at the Global Wellness Institute, tells Axios.

- The nonprofit named " [analog wellness](https://www.globalwellnesssummit.com/global-wellness-trends-2/) " a top trend for 2025.

**The bottom line:** The" [ludicrously capacious bag](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CJUoQMNbA0g) " just got a wellness rebrand.
